## Leak 2 — root cause (investigated post-run)

**Not a hydration bug.** `hasListUnsubscribe` is hydrated and read correctly. The brickellpay 2.0% drop rate has two compounding causes:

1. **Config gap.** brickellpay runs on the business-default `bulkSignalThreshold: 2`. Both sibling business accounts carry an account-level override `bulkSignalThreshold: 1` (healthcarema, summitmiami). brickellpay never got it.
2. **Design smell.** On the Outlook fetch path only one of `detectBulkSignals`' five signals is hydrated — `list-unsubscribe`. `precedence`, `toRecipients/ccRecipients` (→bcc), and `gmailCategories` are never populated (build-bundle fetches `internetMessageHeaders` but extracts only List-Unsubscribe). So a bulk business email caps at **score 1**, which can never reach **threshold 2**. The business default of 2 is structurally unreachable on Outlook — which is why the other two business accounts had to override to 1.

**Evidence:**
- Config: `brickellpay.bulkSignalThreshold = (default→2)`; `healthcarema = 1`; `summitmiami = 1`.
- Bundle: **239 of 1177 brickellpay survivors (20.3%) carry `hasListUnsubscribe=true`** (SEAA conference vendors: hellovivid, payzli, luqra, mbncard, nmi.com…). At threshold 1 these would have classified `"ignore"` → heuristic candidates (`"ignore"` ∈ business `deletionPolicy.categories`). They are survivors ⇒ threshold 2 is the confirmed blocker.
- microsoft (3/440), cardpointe (0/52), github (6/50) mostly lack List-Unsubscribe entirely — not addressable by threshold; these are `alwaysDelete`-shaped (= Leak 1).

**Fix options (design decision pending):**
- **A — Quick config alignment:** add `bulkSignalThreshold: 1` to brickellpay (mirrors both siblings). Reclassifies the 239 LU survivors → heuristic candidates (reviewed, *not* auto-deleted). 1-line, low-risk.
- **B — Fix the default:** change business-type default to `bulkSignalThreshold: 1` (the two overrides become redundant; applies to all business accounts).
- **C — Make threshold 2 meaningful:** hydrate `precedence` + `To/Cc` from the already-fetched `internetMessageHeaders` in build-bundle's Outlook path, so genuinely-bulk mail can legitimately reach score ≥2 without lowering the bar.

A is the immediate unblock; C is the principled fix (keeps a 2-signal bar but actually feeds it). They compose.

### Fix applied: A + C — outcome (re-run `--since 30d`)

Both shipped: `bulkSignalThreshold: 1` added to brickellpay (config A); build-bundle's Outlook fetch now hydrates `precedence` + `To/Cc` via a new exported `mapOutlookMessage()` (code C, TDD'd — 3 new tests, suite 260→263 green).

| metric | before | after | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| brickellpay survivors | 1177 | 739 | **−438** |
| brickellpay candidates | 125 | 562 | +437 |
| brickellpay survivors w/ List-Unsubscribe | 239 | 30* | −209 |
| brickellpay explicit-dropped | 26 | 26 | 0 |
| **total reasoning units R** | **3239** | **3234** | **−5** |

\* the 30 residual are protected senders (prioritySenders/neverDelete) that correctly skip the bulk check. Fix A caught the 239 List-Unsubscribe emails; fix C's precedence/bcc signals caught ~199 more that carried no List-Unsubscribe.

**Key finding — Leak 2 is a correctness fix, not a cost fix.** R barely moved (−5). Both survivors *and* candidates are reasoned (`build-bundle.js` feeds both tags into `groupForReasoning`), so reclassifying survivor→candidate doesn't reduce reasoning load. What it fixes: 438 bulk emails that were silently surviving as FYI (never offered for deletion) are now correctly in the deletion-candidate lane. **R is moved only by explicit-drop (alwaysDelete/scam) and collapse — i.e. Leak 1.** Leak 2's fix is the *prerequisite*: those 438 were invisible to deletion review as survivors; now they're visible and promotable to `alwaysDelete`, which is where the actual cost reduction comes from. The "explicit-dropped suspiciously low" framing of the gotcha was a true symptom but a different mechanism — explicit-dropped is driven purely by `alwaysDelete`/`scamPatterns` config maturity, never by bulk signals.
